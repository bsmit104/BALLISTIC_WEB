THE PROCESS

It all started with a pitch.

The enigmatic Caterpillar from Alice in Wonderland, known for his laid-back demeanor and penchant for philosophical conversations, would likely order at Taco Bell with an air of detached curiosity. In a languid drawl, he might say, "My dear Taco Bell attendant, I find myself in a state of heightened curiosity regarding your culinary creations. Pray, offer me your most peculiar assortment of tacos, for I wish to embark on a gastronomic journey that transcends the ordinary. A Chalupa Supreme, perhaps, or perchance a Crunchwrap Supreme – the very names stir a sense of wonder within me. Let us not forget the mysteries of the sauce packets, each containing its own nugget of existential wisdom. I implore you to guide me through this surreal feast, for I am in search of flavors that will transport me to a realm beyond the mundane."

LO-FI PROTOTYPING

Scene Flow

Woody, the rootin'-tootin' cowboy from Toy Story, would stride into McDonald's with a confident swagger, his cowboy hat tipping ever so slightly. Approaching the counter, he'd flash a friendly grin and say, "Howdy, partners! I reckon I've been roundin' up toys all day, and this cowboy's got a hankerin' for some grub. I'll take a Big Mac, hold the pickles, just like they do it in the Wild West. And, of course, a side of them golden fries – the finest spuds this side of Andy's room. Y'all got any root beer? Nothing beats a cold sarsaparilla after a day on the frontier. Partner, let's make it quick; there's a whole bunch of toys back at the ranch waitin' for Sheriff Woody to come back with a full belly!"
